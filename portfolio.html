A portrait doesn’t have to be specific or show the persons whole face. Sometimes, a detail is enough, to transport an idea of the person who is behind. With little tools we can show the viewer what the person is like or what she likes. But also the perspective of the photography itself does tell us how he wants to show us his chosen subject. By the objects placed in the picture, you can ask yourself many questions. Is it a cold, heartless person or an outgoing one? What are her hobbies? Does she carry something valuable for herself with her all the time? What is it? What’s her favorite color and what food does she like? Is there maybe a feeling of imprisonment for the person or how does she feel? This and more question you can ask by just looking at a portrait, and without asking it tells you even more.

Nature is all around us. The plants and animals live in perfect harmony together, all decided by the nature’s behavior. Even a simple grass may fight his way through a stone and take its place in the little pit. Stones in all colors and shapes, trees and shrubs take over the human objects. Spiders adapt their webs to the ropes, the bush grows around the chandelier. In combination with light and shadow the arrangement looks fascinating and perfect to be captured on camera.

One of the first steps in getting to know the program Cinema4D is doing a tutorial or taking a class. For me, I tried to put some simple shapes together and worked with different surfaces and materials. What I didn’t know before and only found out at the rendering process, glass needs a long time to render. It’s needs more power as if it would be a simple woodcube. In addition, I had many balls and a glassbowl as well. But the result was worth it, the reflections and the multiple colors look stunning.

In Vienna, I was busy collecting flyers, brochures and impressions. To capture the time in Vienna, I created two collages with the collected material and the Austrian newspaper "der Standard" as base. I made sure that both fit together in terms of color and content. On the template, which I made from one of my photos, I sprayed in the other color on the collages, to give them one more thing in common. While one of the two collages occur dark and mysterious, the other one appears bright and loud. It should show two sides of Vienna, the one full of life with the antique buildings and the happy tourists. On the other hand, I wandered through the small filthy alleys, filled with trash and I saw the beggars in the corner of the subway. The Collage expresses my mixed feelings from Vienna and the situation, who certainly isn’t like this only in Vienna.

The silence is a confusing sound. Especially if it’s not even a sound. The silence can have a acalming effect, but when time flies by, it can also drive you crazy. I sat down on the floor and captured the silent room. I paid special attention to the incidence of light to give the pictures a special feeling. Except the emptiness and silence, the picture radiates a summery, cosy and warm feeling. One, in which one likes to stay longer and simply say nothing. Enjoying the silence for a bit and forget the outside. This gives the silence a sound of its own, which we all decide by ourselves.

Ink

The use of ink is often associated with tradition artwork and techniques. With my work, I connected past and present. While I worked with brushes, feathers, water and ink, the objects shown have modern aspects in it. One is holding a smartphone, the other is climbing in a dreamcatcher, which you can find for example in an adventure park. The technique to work first with brushes, glaze the ink with much water and let it dry worked really good for me. Only after the first layer is dry, I could add details with the feather and undiluted ink.
